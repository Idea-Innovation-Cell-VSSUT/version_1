Are Science and art antithetical to each other?

Pradipta Sathpathy

Science has a cold clinical approach which kills the joy and wonders of life, some say. Not quite. Its true that the rainbow is merely white light broken into spectrum and the moon is an aggregate of rocks. But does that really deaden our appreciation of the bright colors of the rainbow or does that kill our ability to enjoy Wordsworth and Shelley. That doesn't of course.

Reducing science to bare reasons is pretty much an injustice to it.
The human mind creates art as well as the laws of universe and nature. Neither is possible without a spirit of imagination.

Picasso's portraits are indeed considered as space-time coordination in paintings. Most of our reputed scientists were artists as well. Einstein was a great musician. These are some of the best illustrations of harmony of science and art.
Life is a many splendoured entity. Reality has many planes. Science and art far from being antithetical, are parts of the same reality but have different perspectives. Compartmentalizing them within narrow boundaries would be detrimental to human welfare. There are no fine lines that can demarcate the end of art and begining of science.
